Impact of Diet

Recent studies have identified a link between the consumption of ultra-processed foods and an increased risk of type 2 diabetes. Specifically, savory snacks, processed meats, ready-to-eat meals, and sweetened beverages have been associated with a higher incidence of the condition.

8 January 2025

Eating Well & Managing Diabetes | ADA

The American Diabetes Association (ADA) provides comprehensive guidelines on healthy eating for diabetes management. The ADA recommends the Diabetes Plate Method, which involves filling half the plate with non-starchy vegetables, a quarter with lean proteins, and a quarter with quality carbohydrates. This approach helps in maintaining balanced nutrition and controlling blood sugar levels.

Dark Chocolate Consumption May Reduce Type 2 Diabetes Risk

Regular dark chocolate consumption may lower type 2 diabetes risk by 21%, thanks to its antioxidants and flavonoids that improve insulin sensitivity. Moderation is key to avoid excess calories.

Research from the Centenary Institute has identified specific blood fats that can predict how effectively individuals with prediabetes will improve their blood sugar levels through diet and weight loss.
